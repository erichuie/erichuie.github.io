About Me

My first introduction into technology was when my parents got me a Game Boy Advance for Christmas. I was obsessed with the device and how entertaining the games were and was always wondering how the screen could seemingly create something out of nothing. As I kept growing, I saw the Game Boy Advance turn into the Nintendo Switch, the CRT TVs turn into HD LCD screen TVs, and the big, boxy Apple Macintosh computers turn into smaller form yet more powerful MacBooks. And with each evolution of technology, I became more excited by each device's array of endless features and wanted to learn more about how I could be involved with creating such features. So I went to college at McGill University and graduated in 2020 with a Bachelor's Degree in Software Engineering. After some setbacks caused from the Covid-19 pandemic, I started my first job as a Quality Assurance Engineer at the company Saviynt, which lasted for one year. I then wanted to look for a developer focused role and retrain my functional skills so I attended a web development bootcamp at Rithm School. My goal is to become a Backend Software Developer focused in developing Web Applications.
